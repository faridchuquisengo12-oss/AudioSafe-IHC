75–85 dB recorded by Lima on main avenues — well above the recommended limit of 53 dB (WHO). Hearing damage is silent and cumulative.

Invisible damage

Noisy cities

FAQ

Answers to your questions before you start

Do I need a special device to measure noise?

No. AudioSafe uses your smartphone's built-in microphone to measure sound levels. No additional hardware is required.

How accurate are the measurements?

Measurements have an accuracy of ±2–3 dB compared to professional sound level meters. For clinical or legal contexts, we recommend supplementing with certified equipment.

Does AudioSafe help comply with SUNAFIL regulations?

Yes. Exportable daily exposure records and verifiable history help demonstrate occupational risk monitoring during inspections.

Is my location and health data private?

Yes. Your personal and location data is never shared with third parties without your explicit consent. Collaborative map data is shared anonymously and in aggregate.

Can I use AudioSafe as a health professional for my patients?

Yes. The professional segment includes a patient panel with exposure history, exportable reports, and longitudinal analysis designed for clinical use.

Does it work in areas without internet access?

AudioSafe can capture data offline and sync it once you regain connection. The collaborative map requires a connection to update other users' data.
